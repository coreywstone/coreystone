Practice Expectations

Attitude

  1. Stay positive - encourage others and yourself.
  2. When you get frustrated, focus your energy on how you can improve the situation.
  3. If you have an inappropriate outburst of frustration or anger, you get to run three laps to think about how your outbursts help or hurt yourself and the squad.
  4. Handle problems with each other directly and professionally. Don't talk behind people's backs.
  5. "Own" the skill or routine you're working on - if it falls, don't blame others - concentrate on what you can do to make it better.
  6. If your attitude is hurting the squad, you'll be kicked out of practice. You can come back when your attitude is improved.
  7. Life is 10% what happens to you and 90% how you respond to it - respond wisely - be an "improver," not a coper or quitter.
  8. Be confident - expect success, expect to win, expect adversity and overcome it.
  9. Swearing gets you 25 (your choice of push-ups or crunches).
  10. No excuses!

Efficiency

  1. Be taped, stretching, and listening to the coaches the minute practice is scheduled to begin.
  2. When a coach yells "Come In," run in immediately and do not talk.
  3. Listen when a coach or captain is speaking to the squad.
  4. If a coach is around when a skill comes down, don't talk until the coach has spoken.
  5. Have a sense of urgency when stunting and tumbling; rest as little as possible between attempts; quickly throw about five in a row then rest.
  6. If you need to have an in-depth talk with a coach, do it either after practice, well before practice, or by email.

Safety

  1. Spot aggressively and catch as high as possible.
  2. No jewelry or hats.
  3. No unnecessary talking when pyramids or stunts are up.
  4. If you're injured, see the trainers and follow their instructions precisely.
  5. Everyone does 25 if a girl hits the floor.
