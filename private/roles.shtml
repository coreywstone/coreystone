Squad Roles

Leadership team: Zach, Evan, Kaitlin, Anna, Taia, Kelly

Captains and leaders should exemplify the squad's values and ideals; their attitude and work ethic should be a role model for how every squad member should act. A good test is to ask, "if everyone had the same attitude and work ethic as the captains, how successful would the squad be?" (Note that the captains' skill level is largely irrelevant.) Their responsibilities include:

  • Help the coaches lead the squad, both vocally and by example.
  • If coaches aren't present, the captains will be in charge and should direct the squad.
  • Help solve problems within the squad before they become big problems.
  • Act as a liaison between coaches and squad members by a) informing coaches of potential conflicts and issues within the squad, and b) helping the squad understand the coaches' intentions.
  • Call stunts and pyramids on the field/court if the coach hasn't called them.
Recommended reading: "The 21 Irrefutable Laws of Leadership," and "The Leader in You" (see book list)

Equipment Managers: Guy Captains

Ensure that we have all our equipment (megs, signs, flags) when and where we need it, and that it's stored properly when we don't need it.
